Chameleon

  1. Chameleon are reptiles that are a part of the iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons have excellent haring abilities.

Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Cicadas

  1. Cicadas are like tiny violins.
  2. Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
  3. Their enemies are the stuff of nightmares.

Pandas

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  3. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.

Teddy Bear

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  2. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
  3. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
